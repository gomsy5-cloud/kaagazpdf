How to Compress a PDF to 50 KB or 100 KB for Online Forms

Updated July 2026 · 6 min read · KaagazPDF Guides

Almost every Indian government form — SSC, UPSC, Railway RRB, bank exams, passport, scholarship portals — sets a strict file-size limit for the documents you upload. The most common limits are 50 KB, 100 KB, 200 KB and 500 KB. If your PDF is even slightly over the limit, the portal rejects it. This guide explains exactly why PDFs get large and how to shrink one to an exact size in under a minute, for free, without your file ever leaving your phone or computer.

Why is my PDF so large?

A PDF made from a phone scan or a photo is heavy because it stores a full-resolution image of every page. A single 12-megapixel photo can be 3–8 MB on its own. When you place two or three such photos into a PDF, the file easily crosses 10 MB — far above a 100 KB form limit. Text-only PDFs (like a downloaded admit card) are usually small already; it is the scanned and photographed documents that need compressing.

The two ways to compress a PDF

There are two honest approaches, and knowing the difference saves you a lot of frustration:

  • Structural optimisation (keeps text): the tool cleans up the internal structure of the PDF without touching quality. This is great for PDFs that were exported from Word or a website, but it does very little for scanned documents because the bulk is the image.
  • Image downsampling (biggest shrink): the tool re-renders each page at a lower resolution and re-compresses the images. This is what actually takes a 5 MB scan down to under 100 KB. The text stays perfectly readable on screen and on print — you simply don't need 300 DPI for a form upload.
Rule of thumb: for a scanned or photographed document, always choose the "Extreme / biggest shrink" option. For a clean text PDF, "Recommended" is enough.

Step-by-step: compress a PDF to under 100 KB

  1. Open the KaagazPDF Compress tool on your phone or computer.

Is it safe to compress sensitive documents online?

This is a real concern, because these files often contain your Aadhaar number, marks, and photograph. Most online compressors upload your document to their servers, process it there, and (they say) delete it later. KaagazPDF is built differently: the compression runs entirely inside your own browser using in-browser technology, so the file never leaves your device. You can even switch off your internet after the page has loaded and it will still work.
